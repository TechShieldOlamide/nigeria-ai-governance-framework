# Nigeria AI Governance Framework

A practical AI governance framework for Nigerian businesses building, deploying, or procuring AI systems. Mapped to the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA), the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) AI review announced June 2026, the EU AI Act, and ISO 42001.

## Why This Exists

The NDPC announced in June 2026 that it is reviewing the NDPA 2023 specifically to address artificial intelligence, automated decision making, robotics, and big data. The regulator's position is clear: AI is already subject to Nigerian data protection law through existing provisions on automated decision making and profiling, and stricter, more targeted rules are coming.

Most Nigerian businesses building or using AI products have not started thinking about governance. This toolkit helps them start.

## Who This Is For

- Nigerian fintechs using AI for credit scoring, fraud detection, or customer profiling
- African SaaS companies building AI-powered products for local or global markets
- Compliance professionals advising clients on AI risk and governance
- Founders integrating AI tools into their products or operations
- Product and engineering teams who need to understand their compliance obligations before they ship

## The Regulatory Reality

The NDPA 2023 already applies to AI systems through:

- **Section 25:** Requires a lawful basis for processing personal data, including data processed by AI systems
- **Section 27:** Requires organisations to inform individuals about automated decision making and profiling
- **Section 37:** Grants individuals the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing when those decisions have legal or significant consequences
- **DPIA requirement:** High-risk AI processing requires a Data Protection Impact Assessment before deployment

The NDPC has signalled that stricter, AI-specific rules are coming. Organisations that build governance frameworks now will be ahead of the enforcement curve.
